## Current View Of The Waseda Problem

The most useful framing is still:

- quiet operating band
- transient departures from that band
- recovery
- spacing / clustering
- slow drift across the block

This framing feels materially better than:

- dense raw event counting
- or broad claims about low animation as a state by itself

The current figures are useful because they show actual extracted patterns, not
just a conceptual cartoon.

## Important Boundary To Keep

Do not let the presence of a reproducible MATLAB pipeline upgrade the claim by
accident.

The pipeline is now more portable and easier to continue, but the data are
still:

- small
- preliminary
- heterogeneous across subjects
- weakly anchored in task timing

So the scientific status remains candidate-pattern / protocol-development.

## What Feels Most Durable

Three things now feel genuinely worth carrying across sessions:

1. `sub1` video drift remains the clearest concrete example.
2. Artifact-screened departures are more interpretable than dense derivative
   bursts.
3. The interval and shape summaries are useful descriptive objects even before
   they justify any psychological label.

## What Still Feels Fragile

- exact burst counts are threshold-sensitive
- desk versus video contrasts are visible but not strong enough to trust
  naively
- histogram appearance can mislead quickly if binning is lazy
- some events are probably posture regulation or comfort adjustments rather
  than anything cognitive
