## Regime Definitions

- candidate window: `[walkB - 5 s, walkB)`
- thresholded on dynamic-motion proxy
- threshold `< 40`
- fill above-threshold gaps `<= 0.1 s`
- require low-animation runs `>= 0.5 s`

### Sustained walking
- candidate window: `[walkB, walkFinish]`
- thresholded on dynamic-motion proxy
- threshold `> 100`
- fill below-threshold gaps `<= 0.25 s`
- require walking runs `>= 1.0 s`

## Why These Choices Happened

- Raw magnitude looked visually cleaner for walking waveform shape.
- Dynamic envelope looked much better for separating quiet standing from anticipatory motion.
- However, raw-summary walking vigor flattened the pre-walk vs walking relation almost completely.
- Dynamic-summary walking vigor preserved a modest negative association.

## Later Session Addendum: What Actually Mattered

### 1. The BH3-first approach was good engineering, but not the scientific endpoint
- `bh3.acc` was the right place to prototype:
  - browser controls,
  - regime logic,
  - trace auditing,
  - and low-animation / walking mask semantics.
- But it was not the strongest sensor for the clip-affect question.
- Once the analysis switched to `front STb`, the clip-view effect became much
  clearer.

### 2. Sensor choice overwhelmed several earlier disagreements
- A lot of apparent conflict between "weak effect" and "Claude found
  something" was explained by using different sensors and different motion
  definitions.
- The useful affect-related signal is not "any accelerometer summary"; it is
  much more specific to sternum front-STb low-animation clip motion.

### 3. The clip effect is stronger than the pre-walk story
- The pre-walk -> walk question never became as convincing as the clip-view
  question.
- It is still worth keeping as a side analysis, but it should not drive the
  narrative.

### 4. The sign question matters less than the informativeness question
- When the conversation shifted toward grant figures, the relevant message
  became:
  - "does motion carry information?"
- not:
  - "is every physiology feature signed in the same direction?"
- This is why the absolute-beta radar / rose representation became acceptable.

### 5. Mixed models were the right stabilizer
- Subject-centering and pooled correlations were useful diagnostics only.
- The cleaner inferential stance came from within-/between-subject mixed
  models.
- Keep using that framing for any serious comparison to physiology.

### 6. Nonlinearity concern was valid
- Subject-profile inspection showed:
  - many positive high-vs-low valence differences,
  - but heterogeneous subject-wise shapes.
- Therefore:
  - report a positive within-subject trend,
  - but avoid implying that every subject obeys the same linear trajectory.

### 7. Physiology is not empty, just weaker and more fragmented
- Breathing, BVP variability, EDA variability, and HR had some signal.
- HRV-like interval features were available and testable.
- But none displaced front-STb clip motion as the main effect.

### 8. Figure lesson
- Radar / rose figures are acceptable only when treated as summary glyphs.
- They should not be the only inferential display.
- Pair them later with one straightforward coefficient figure or compact table.
